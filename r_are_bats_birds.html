The Bible Calls Bats Birds.

Claim

The Bible includes bats in a list of unclean birds. Bats are mammals, not birds. So the Bible contains an error.

Passage

Leviticus 11:13-19

Short Response

Birds/Fowl is a mistranslation. It should say flying creatures which would include bats.

Long Response

This is a mistranslated term. The original Hebrew word "עוף" (oph) refers to "flying creatures" in general, which would include bats.1 The following verse in this same chapter (Leviticus 11:20) uses the same word "עוף" (oph) to refer to flying insects!2 Did the ancient Hebrews really think that insects were birds? Of course not. They simply meant flying creatures in general.

Objections

Objection: "The Hebrew says "העוף" (ha-oph) not "עוף" (oph)"

Response: The prefix "ה" (ha) is the definite article "the" in Hebrew. It does not change the meaning of the word it is attached to. So "העוף" (ha-oph) simply means "the flying creatures". The meaning remains the same.3
